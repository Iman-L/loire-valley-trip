Day One

Chateau Royal d'Amboise
Visit

Day Two

Chenonceau — Day & Night

The signature day. See the most romantic chateau in France twice: once in the morning calm, and again at night when the gardens are illuminated with classical music and you'll have the place almost entirely to yourselves.

9:30 AM — Morning Visit
Chateau de Chenonceau
Visit
★★★★★ 4.7 · 48,534 reviews

Arrive right at opening for the quietest experience. The gallery stretching across the River Cher is breathtaking. Explore the interiors, the flower arrangements (Chenonceau is famous for them), and the formal gardens of Diane de Poitiers and Catherine de Medici. In July you can also take a boat trip on the Cher beneath the arches — a uniquely romantic experience.

Chenonceaux (25 min drive from Amboise)Open 9:30am–6:30pm (July)Pre-book timed entry online
1:00 PM — Afternoon
Chateau de Villandry Gardens
Visit
★★★★★ 4.7 · 19,978 reviews

July is absolute peak bloom. Six levels of Renaissance gardens with 1,000+ lime trees, ornamental flower beds, a water garden, and the famous geometric vegetable garden. Villandry is naturally less crowded than Chenonceau and Chambord, making it a welcome contrast. Many visitors rank these gardens above every other chateau they see in the Loire.

Villandry (40 min from Amboise)Open 9am–7pm (July)
Picnic idea: Pick up fresh bread, cheese, olives, fruit and pastries from a boulangerie and eat in the grounds — a perfectly romantic halal-friendly lunch with garden views.
After dark — Return Visit
Chenonceau Night Garden Promenade
After Dark

This is the highlight of the entire trip. On summer evenings, Chenonceau opens its gardens for a nocturnal promenade — the chateau illuminated over the river, the gardens lit with soft lighting, and the music of Corelli drifting through the air. The crowds are gone. It's just you, the gardens, the river, and the lit-up Renaissance arches. Genuinely one of the most romantic things you can do in France.

Summer only: The Nocturnal Promenades run on select evenings in July and August. Check chenonceau.com for exact dates. Arrive as it gets dark (~10pm). This experience is completely different from the daytime visit.
Day Three

Chambord, Cycling & the Blois Light Show

The grandest chateau first thing, then a lazy riverside cycle along the Loire in the afternoon warmth, and a spectacular 360-degree light show projected onto a castle courtyard after dark.

9:00 AM — First In
Chateau de Chambord
Visit
★★★★★ 4.6 · 61,961 reviews

Arrive at 9am when the doors open — you'll have at least 90 minutes before it gets busy. The double-helix staircase (two intertwined staircases where people going up never meet those coming down) is attributed to da Vinci. Climb to the rooftop terrace for sweeping views of the 5,440-hectare park. The morning light here is extraordinary.

Chambord (50 min from Amboise)Open 9am–6pmArrive at opening for quietest visit
2:00 PM — Afternoon
Loire a Velo — Riverside Cycling
Cycle

Back in Amboise, rent bikes and cycle the Loire a Velo route along the river. It's flat, well-marked, and beautiful in July — fields of sunflowers, quiet river views, and barely a car in sight. Cycle east towards Chaumont-sur-Loire or west towards Tours. This is the antidote to chateau crowds: just you, the bikes, and the river.

Bike hire available in Amboise centreLoire a Velo route — flat and easy
Route idea: Amboise to Chaumont-sur-Loire is about 20km each way along the river. Stop at Chaumont's International Garden Festival (on in July) for extraordinary contemporary garden installations — a hidden gem most tourists miss.
7:00 PM — Explore Blois
Old Town Blois & Dinner
Explore

Drive to Blois (35 min) for the evening. Wander the steep cobbled lanes of the old town, grab dinner from a creperie (buckwheat galettes with egg, cheese, mushrooms — naturally halal, just say "sans porc") or find a kebab spot near the centre. Then head to the chateau courtyard.

10:30 PM — The Show
Chateau de Blois Son et Lumiere
After Dark

A 45-minute 360-degree video projection mapped onto all four facades of the chateau courtyard. Walls collapse, Catherine de Medici appears, the Duke of Guise is more alive than ever — love stories, tragedies and secrets projected in immersive detail. Narrated by famous French actors with English translation via headsets. This is something you can only see in summer, and it's spectacular.

Chateau Royal de Blois10:30pm start (July)English headsets available
July bonus: The late 10:30pm start means you have a full evening in Blois first. The show runs April–September. Book combined chateau + show tickets online at chateaudeblois.fr (available from March 2026).
Day Four

Da Vinci's Home & Farewell Lunch

A relaxed final morning at Leonardo da Vinci's last home — an intimate, personal visit without the grand-chateau crowds — then a farewell lunch at a wonderful Syrian restaurant in Tours before heading home.

9:30 AM — Visit
Chateau du Clos Luce
Visit
★★★★★ 4.5 · 23,381 reviews

Where Leonardo da Vinci lived his final three years (1516–1519). See his bedroom, studio, and kitchen, then explore the gardens filled with life-size models of his inventions — flying machines, bridges, tanks. This is the most personal and intimate of the chateaux: less grand, far more moving. The gardens alone are worth an hour in the July warmth.

2 Rue du Clos Luce, AmboiseOpen 9am–7pm (July)
12:00 PM — Market Browse
Amboise Friday Market
Explore

If your last day falls on a Friday (or Sunday), catch the morning market along the Loire. Fresh produce, local cheeses, pastries, honey, and crafts. Less hectic than the Sunday market. Pick up some goodies for the road or as gifts to take home.

Halal Dining Guide

Eating Well in the Loire Valley

Honest advice: Amboise is a small French town with limited halal restaurants. But with a bit of planning, you'll eat very well.

Your Food Strategy

Halal Restaurants

Amboise: Power One Sushi (halal Japanese/Asian, closed Mon & Sun). Tours (25 min): Alep (Syrian, 4.5★), plus halal kebab and Middle Eastern spots on Rue Colbert.

Seafood at French Restaurants

Loire restaurants serve excellent fish. At any French restaurant, fish mains are safe — just ask "sans sauce au vin" (no wine sauce) and "sans porc" (no pork).

Self-Catering

Your B&B has a guest kitchen. Amboise market (Fri & Sun mornings) has incredible produce. Super U stocks halal meat. Buy fresh bread daily from any boulangerie.

Creperies

Buckwheat galettes with egg, cheese, mushrooms are naturally halal. Found everywhere. Just say "sans jambon, sans porc." A reliable option across Amboise, Blois, and Tours.

Practical Details

July Travel Tips & Booking

July is peak season but these tips will help you enjoy it rather than endure it.

Transport

Drive via Eurotunnel from Folkestone — most flexible option and you'll want a car for the chateaux. Or Eurostar + TGV + car hire in Tours.

July Tips

Weather

25–32°C, long sunny days. Sunset ~9:45pm. Pack sunscreen, hats, and light layers for air-conditioned chateau interiors.

Beat the Crowds

Visit chateaux at opening (9–9:30am) or late afternoon (after 5pm). Use evenings for the night experiences — that's when July really shines. Weekdays are much quieter than weekends.

Prayer Facilities

Nearest mosque: Mosquee de Tours (Rue du Colombier, ~25 min). For Jumu'ah on Friday, route through Tours. Prayer room available in Tours city centre.

Pass'Chateaux

A combined pass covering Blois, Chambord, Amboise, and Clos Luce saves money and often includes skip-the-line. Available at bloischambord.co.uk.

Getting There from Kent

Option A — Drive

Folkestone → Eurotunnel (35 min) → Calais → A26/A28 → Amboise. Total ~5 hours door to door. Most flexible — you'll want a car anyway.

Option B — Train

St Pancras → Paris Nord (Eurostar, 1h15) → Paris Montparnasse (metro) → Tours (TGV, 1h) → hire a car in Tours for the chateaux.
